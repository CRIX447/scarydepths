⟡ THE VHS MANIFEST ⟡

SCARY DEPTHS is a first-person analog horror experience that blends retro VHS aesthetics with psychological dread. Set in an abandoned underwater research station, you uncover a series of corrupted tapes that reveal a government experiment gone wrong. The year is 1987. Your only equipment: a failing sonar, a portable CRT monitor, and a voice recorder that catches whispers that shouldn't exist.

Each session generates a unique "depth layer" — rooms shift, hallways stretch, and the same looped broadcast hides messages meant to drive you mad. The game features no combat; instead, you must hide, solve environmental puzzles, and manage your sanity by avoiding prolonged exposure to static entities. The audio design is crucial: every creak, distorted melody, and reversed voice clue leads you closer to the truth — or to your second death.

🎞️ "I saw the tape. It showed me things. The depths have eyes. And they are laughing." — recovered log #42

The game also features a unique "replay corruption" system: each time you restart, the VHS glitches differently, new rooms emerge, previous safe spots become hazards. CRIX STUDIOS has designed an audio-driven world where the signal becomes your only companion — a haunting drone that reacts to your proximity to the entity.
